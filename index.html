So Therefore
"So" is used to show what happens because of something. It is usually used in everyday speech and is easy to understand. "Therefore" is a little more formal and sounds like something we say when we are explaining something carefully. It also shows the result of something, but it is often used in writing or when we want to sound more serious.
We use "so" to connect the reason and the result in a simple way. We use "therefore" to explain why something happened, often in a clear and thoughtful way.
